USPS "Thank you" Stamps

0.91 x 1.19 in.

A harmonious blend of classic and modern design, Dana Tanamachi's "Thank You" stamps for the USPS celebrate the universal language of gratitude. The elegant, flowing script, inspired by historical penmanship and Art Nouveau, evokes a sense of timeless beauty. The intricate floral details, reminiscent of William Morris's textile designs, symbolize growth and unity. Each stamp, with its unique color palette, offers a visual delight, making every "Thank You" a heartfelt gesture.

Starbucks 50th Anniversary Poster

30 x 25 in.

Dana Tanamachi's commemorative poster for Starbucks' 50th Anniversary beautifully captures the essence of the brand and its roots. Inspired by the iconic Lady Liberty, the central siren symbolizes strength, resilience, and heritage. She is gracefully surrounded by Pacific Northwest flora, creating a natural frame that pays homage to the region where Starbucks was born. This artwork is both a celebration of Starbucks' legacy and a tribute to the unique culture and landscape that have shaped its journey over five decades.

Time Magazine Cover "Reinventing College"

Oct. 29, 2012

Dana Tanamachi's uses her signature hand-lettering style to encapsulate the spirit of transformation in higher education. The cover art features intricate chalk typography, symbolizing both tradition and innovation. Through bold, detailed letters, it reflects the evolving landscape of college education, emphasizing the need for adaptability and rethinking established systems in a rapidly changing world.

The Oprah Magazine

February 2012

Dana Tanamachi brought fresh, imaginative flair to O, The Oprah Magazine's February issue, breaking away from the usual Valentine's Day motifs. Given free rein to "Express yourself," Tanamachi filled the cover with her own inspired doodles, resulting in a standout design. Her work was so well-received that she was invited to create additional artwork for the magazine's “You In Six Words” feature, guiding readers to craft their own life mantras. Tanamachi’s presence at the photoshoot allowed her to make real-time adjustments, and her process was later spotlighted in the magazine’s “Behind the Scenes” column.

Sephora AAPI Heritage Month

In celebration of AAPI Heritage Month, Dana Tanamachi created a beautiful illustration for Sephora that honors the rich diversity within the Asian American and Pacific Islander community. As a Japanese/Mexican American artist, Tanamachi incorporated vibrant colors, intricate patterns, and cultural symbols, capturing the unique blend of traditions, stories, and heritage. The artwork reflects unity and pride, serving as a tribute to the many voices and histories celebrated during May.
